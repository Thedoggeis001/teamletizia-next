    <section class="article-body">
        <p>
            <strong>Imperium Europae: Dies Tristis</strong> is a narrative visual novel set in
            contemporary Europe, seen through the eyes of two figures who no longer belong to it.
            Tibor, an Austro-Hungarian boar and former imperial infantry officer, and Straus,
            a Habsburg eagle of aristocratic lineage, awaken in a world that has forgotten the one
            that defined them.
        </p>

        <p>
            Modern cities rise where empires once stood. Languages have changed, symbols have faded,
            and memory has been reduced to museum labels and decorative history. What Tibor and Straus
            encounter is not only physical ruin, but a deeper collapse: the loss of meaning.
        </p>

        <h2 class="section-title">✨ Chapter I: The Europe That Was</h2>
        <p>
            The story begins in Vienna at dawn, among construction sites, advertising screens,
            and unfamiliar flags. The empire they served no longer exists, and Europe itself
            seems unable—or unwilling—to offer them a place.
        </p>

        <p>
            Their journey moves through Vienna, Budapest, Trieste, Sarajevo, and Brussels.
            Each city represents a different wound: memory turned into spectacle, fragmented
            nationalism, liquid identity, unresolved trauma, and bureaucracy without a soul.
        </p>

        <blockquote class="pink-blockquote">
            “What remains of an identity when the world that defined it no longer exists?”
        </blockquote>

        <h2 class="section-title">🧭 Two Relics, No Homeland</h2>
        <p>
            Tibor survives by adapting. He believes in discipline and order, but no longer in
            the future. Straus, by contrast, refuses to accept a world without hierarchy,
            responsibility, or moral structure. He does not seek to restore the past, but to
            confront the present with what it lacks.
        </p>

        <p>
            Their relationship evolves through dialogue choices that are never right or wrong,
            only revealing. Distance, trust, and ideological fracture grow quietly, often through
            silence rather than open conflict.
        </p>

        <h2 class="section-title">🎭 Themes and Tone</h2>
        <p>
            Imperium Europae: Dies Tristis is not a nostalgic celebration, nor a political manifesto.
            It is an existential drama with subtle political satire, focused on responsibility,
            irrelevance, and the weight of memory in a post-historical Europe.
        </p>

        <blockquote class="pink-blockquote">
            “Empires do not die once. They die again when no one remembers why they mattered.”
        </blockquote>

        <h2 class="section-title">🕹️ Gameplay and Structure</h2>
        <p>
            Gameplay is centered on narrative choices and character relationships.
            Invisible variables shape the story and lead
            to multiple endings, each offering a different response to the same collapse.
        </p>

        <p>
            A single playthrough lasts approximately five to seven hours, with alternative
            routes revealing new perspectives rather than better outcomes.
        </p>

        <h2 class="section-title">📅 Looking Ahead</h2>
        <p>
            Imperium Europae: Dies Tristis is currently in development.
            More information, will be shared in the coming months.
        </p>

        <p>
            This project represents Team Letizia’s commitment to narrative experiences that
            respect the player’s intelligence and emotional engagement—stories meant to linger
            long after the screen goes dark.
        </p>
